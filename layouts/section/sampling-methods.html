Sampling Methods

Gathering information about an entire population often costs too much or is virtually impossible. Instead, we use a sample of the population. A sample should have the same characteristics as the population it is representing.

This section will describe a few of the most common methods: Simple Random Sampling (SRS), Stratified Sampling, Cluster Sampling, Systematic Sampling, and Conveniece Sampling.

Simple random sample

Any group of n individuals is equally likely to be chosen as any other group of n individuals if the simple random sampling technique is used. In other words, each sample of the same size has an equal chance of being selected

T

Technology

Excel example

Simple random sampling is often done using technology. One way to do it is by using the   =RANDARRAY() function in Excel.

It takes 5 arguments   =RANDARRAY([rows], [columns], [min], [max], [whole_number]).

  • rows: The number of rows to be returned
  • columns: The number of columns to be returned
  • min: The minimum number you would like returned
  • max: The maximum number you would like returned
  • whole_number: Return a whole number (1) number or a decimal value (0)

For example,

  • to generate 1 column of 7 whole numbers between 1 and 22, enter  =RANDARRAY(7, 1, 1, 22, 1)
  • to generate 1 column of 10 whole numbers between 50 and 500, enter  =RANDARRAY(10, 1, 50, 500, 1)
  • to generate 1 column of 20 decimals between 100 and 110, enter  =RANDARRAY(20, 1, 100, 110, 0)
  • to generate 2 columns of 10 decimals between 100 and 110, enter  =RANDARRAY(10, 2, 100, 110, 0)

Stratified sample

To choose a stratified sample, divide the population into groups called strata and then take a proportionate number from each stratum. For example, you could stratify (group) your college population by department and then choose a proportionate simple random sample from each stratum (each department) to get a stratified random sample.

Cluster sample

To choose a cluster sample, divide the population into clusters (groups) and then randomly select some of the clusters. All the members from these clusters are in the cluster sample.

E

Example

Cluster Sample

For example, if you randomly sample four departments from your college population, the four departments make up the cluster sample. Divide your college faculty by department. The departments are the clusters. Number each department, and then choose four different numbers using simple random sampling. All members of the four departments with those numbers are the cluster sample.

Systematic Sampling

To choose a systematic sample, randomly select a starting point and take every nth piece of data from a listing of the population.

Systematic Sample

Convenience Sampling

A type of sampling that is non-random is convenience sampling. Convenience sampling involves using results that are readily available.

E

Example

Convenience Sample

For example, a computer software store conducts a marketing study by interviewing potential customers who happen to be in the store browsing through the available software. The results of convenience sampling may be very good in some cases and highly biased (favor certain outcomes) in others.
