Measures of Central Tendency

The "center" of a data set is also a way of describing location. The two most widely used measures of the "center" of the data are the mean (average) and the median.

The median is generally a better measure of the center when there are extreme values or outliers because it is not affected by the precise numerical values of the outliers. The mean is the most common measure of the center.

Another measure of the center is the mode. The mode is the most frequent value. There can be more than one mode in a data set as long as those values have the same frequency and that frequency is the highest. A data set with two modes is called bimodal.

The words " mean" and " average" are often used interchangeably. The substitution of one word for the other is common practice. The technical term is "arithmetic mean," and "average" is technically a center location. However, in practice among non-statisticians, "average" is commonly accepted for "arithmetic mean."

Notation

When you take more advanced Statistics courses, you will see many pairs of symbols used to differentiate between population parameters and sample stiatistics. One such pair is \(\bar{x}\) and \(\mu\).

  • We use \(\bar{x}\) (pronounced “x bar”) to represent the sample mean
  • We use the Greek letter \(\mu\) (pronounced "mew") to represent the population mean.

Example

AIDS patients

AIDS data indicating the number of months a patient with AIDS lives after taking a new antibody drug are as follows (smallest to largest): 3, 4, 8, 8, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 15, 16, 16, 17, 21, 22, 23. Calculate the mean and the median.

sample mean:
$$\bar{x} = \frac{3+4+8 \cdot 2 + 10 + 11 + 12+ 13 + 15 \cdot 2 + 16 \cdot 2 + 1 +21 + 22 + 23 }{17} = \frac{228}{17} = 13.41$$ median:
$$M = 9^{th} number = 14$$

There are 17 numbers in this dataset, so the median is calculated as the "middle" number: 14.
3, 4, 8, 8, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 15, 16, 16, 17, 21, 22, 23

E

Example

Real estate exam tests

Five real estate exam scores are 430, 430, 480, 488, 495, 495. Find the median and the mode.

median: $$M = \frac{3^{rd} number + 4^{th} number}{2} = \frac{480 + 488}{2} = 484$$

The data set is bimodal because the scores 430 and 495 each occur twice.

There are 6 numbers in this dataset, so the median is calculated as the average of the two middle two numbers" 480 and 488
430, 430, 480, 488, 495, 495

When calculating the median, data must be sorted from smallest to largest values.

Averages can be misleading 😨

We often say that the median is resistant to outliers. It means that the median is not influences by outliers as much as the mean.

Outliers are observations that do not fit the rest of the data. They are much lower or much higher than the rest of the data.
